Limits are fundamental in calculus, limit is a term that describes how a function/graph behaves near a specific point. This concept personally drawn my attention to learn more and discuss why was it so important for calculus. Now, I am going to give you some examples to demonstrate some concepts and get more into depth when learning new and confusing concepts.

Suppose the professor posts a graph in the board and asks us to determine the limit at a specific point. Let's see the graph:

Personally, when solving two sided limits, I always place the point in a line and determine the direction of the - and +, so in this way when prof ask me the orientation I know that side to take

Using our orientation, we know the number that the - and + orientation is when getting closer to the point.

If both of our limits are the same, means the limit exists.

Our professor now switches the image, and this is the image now. She asks us to find certain limits.

The first is to divide our both orientations to know which direction the graph gets closer to 2 and pick the negative.

Now pick the positive orientation

If both of our sides are the same, then our limit exists. In this case, our limits are not the same which conducts to a DNE.
