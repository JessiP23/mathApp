Equations with two variables

In this topic we will be using equations into our cartesian system to graph functions. Let's start, suppose my professor gives me the equation y=3x-1. How would you do it?

These are the steps to follow:

  1. Create a table, this table will contain at least 3 points
  2. The coordinates are (-1,-4),(0,-1), and (1,2). Locate those points in a cartesian plane and join them with a line.

    3. This is it. This is how you graph an equation with two variables. I have a question for you, based on this information calculate the graph of the following line 3x + y = 3.

Calculate slope

Slope in the inclination of a line. We can calculate with this method in a few seconds by just looking the graph

Notice that there are two points, this makes easier to find the slope in the line. Now, the line is pointing upwards which means the slope is positive. Then, we find the difference between those two points in the x axis and the y axis.

If you see, the difference between the those two points in the x axis is 2 and the y axis is still 2.

This formula is fundamental because to find the slope is just the division between the difference of those two points in the y axis divided by the difference of x axis. In this case is 2/2 which is equal 1.

Example: Find the slope of the points (3,4) and (5,7).

Slope-Intercept Form

This is the slope intercept formula. y= mx + b

This small formula is really important because is equation of the line when we leave y isolated.

  • m stands for slope
  • b is the y-intercept when x=0

    • In this situation, the slope is given to us. Example: Given m=1/3 and b=4, find the graph

  • Lets find first the y interception. In this case is 4.

    • From that point, use your slope to find the next point. The slope is positive, which means the line is pointing upwards

    According to the example, the slope is 1/3 meaning 1 unit up and three units to the right.

    Join the points. And you have your graph.
