Greatest Common Factor

The GCF is the max number that divides a certain pair of numbers. Example: Find the GCF of 32, 44, 20

The max number that divides these three numbers is 4. Prove it by yourself. This is an idea and your job is to understand why is it 4.

Working with terms is slightly different. For example: Find the GCF of 10x^3y, 5yx, 20x^2

  1. Find GCF of coeficient(numbers)
  2. Focus on each variable that is in those three terms

IMPORTANT

When working with terms including variables, the variable present in all terms is part of the gcf. In this case we have x. To find the correct exponent, the answer is this exponent which is the lowest. The first term has exponent 3, the second is 1, the third is 2.

Finally, include everything in your answer. The GCF of these terms is 5x
