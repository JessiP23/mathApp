It looks similar as the last example solve in last class, the difference is that x has a number next to it. Let's solve it.

  • Place the variable on one side of the = sign. In this case, x is in the left side
  • Isolate x. In this case, we move out any term that does not contain x, in this case is the +5
  • Move +5 to the other side (Remember when transfering any number to the other side is with the opposite sign).
  • If no more terms are in the x side, we can solve the right side.
  • Here is the important part of this. Whenever you see a variable together with a coefficient means they are multiplying each other. So, 3 is multiplying x. To isolate x we need to get rid of 3, knowing some of our rules the way to transfer something to ther other side of the = is by its opposite sign. The opposite of multiplication is division.

This is how we solve our first two-step equation. Practice more examples on your own to master this content.
