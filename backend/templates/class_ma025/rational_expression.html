    Least Common Denominator

    LCD is just the min number multiple of any pair of numbers given to us. Example: Find the LCD of 4 and 5.

    LCD using variables

    To find LCD usign polynomials is indispensable factoring and then figure out what to do from that. There are some special rules about factoring including terms, with next example will cover some basic rules that are used when finding the LCD. Example: Find the LCD of x^2+8x+16 and x^2+3x-4

    Explanation: TPS stands for Trinomial Perfect Square(this is a special product), then factor each term. Based on factorization results, LCD can be deducted. Here are a couple of rules important.

    1. If some terms are similar, choose always the one with the highest exponent
    2. If one term appears in only one term, it is added to the lcd. And thats it.
